## Scene Description and Key Features

For cognitive assessment and picture description tasks, clinicians typically track whether the subject mentions specific key features, actions, and relationships. Below is the breakdown of the generated scene:

### 1. Main Characters and Interaction
* **Older Man:** Sitting at the dining/living room table, wearing glasses and a long-sleeve patterned shirt. He is actively pointing to or moving a piece on the board game.
* **Young Girl:** Sitting opposite the man, wearing braids and a t-shirt. She has a happy/engaged expression and is watching the game.
* **The Game:** A board game laid out on the table with cards, dice, game pieces, and grid squares.

### 2. High-Priority Action Items (Clinical Targets)
Subjects undergoing assessment should ideally identify these concurrent events:
* **The Dog and Tablecloth:** A dog in the bottom-left corner is biting and pulling the corner of the tablecloth. This is causing a vase of flowers to tilt off the table, spilling water.
* **The Smoking Toaster:** On the kitchen counter to the right, a toaster has popped out two burnt, black pieces of toast. A puff of smoke with the word **"POOF!"** is written above it, indicating a minor kitchen mishap.
* **The Cat on the Shelf:** A cat is perched inside an open cabinet/shelf unit above the table. It is reaching out to swat/play with the electric wire of a hanging light bulb.
* **The Postman Outside:** Outside the window on the left, a postman/mail carrier in uniform is riding a tiny toy tractor in the snow, losing control, and slipping backwards with a shocked expression.

### 3. Background and Clutter Details
These items add complexity and test the subject's attention to detail:
* A steaming mug/cup sitting near the toaster.
* Various kitchen utensils (spoons, forks, jars) on the counter.
* A wall clock hanging on the top right.
* Books stacked on the floor to the left, with one open book and loose papers.
* Loose papers/clutter on the floor to the bottom right.
