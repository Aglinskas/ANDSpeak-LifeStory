## Scene Description and Causal Chains (Clinical Targets)

This scene is designed to evaluate a subject's ability to construct narrative sentences, infer cause-and-effect relationships, and notice detail-oriented hazards. The key components include:

### 1. The Distracted Center (The Oblivious Actor)
* **The Father:** Standing in the middle, rolling dough on the kitchen counter. He is wearing headphones and looking intently at a tablet screen. This explains his lack of awareness of the surrounding chaos (a core component of the "distracted parent" trope in tests like Cookie Theft).

### 2. The Spilled Flour Sequence (Left Side)
* **Action:** A young girl is standing on a kitchen chair, holding a large paper bag labeled **"FLOUR"**.
* **Mishap:** She is tilting the bag too far, causing a large stream of flour to pour directly onto the kitchen floor, forming a pile.
* **Inference/Result:** A dog is walking away from the spilled flour, leaving a trail of paw prints leading away from the pile. A subject must connect the dog's path to the flour spill.

### 3. The Unstable Tower & Honey Leak (Right Side)
* **Goal:** A young boy wants to reach a jar of **"HONEY"** placed on a high shelf.
* **Attempt:** He has stacked a tall column of books on a kitchen chair to climb up.
* **Sequence of Mishaps:**
  1. The boy is actively holding onto the stack of books, which is sliding/tilting and about to collapse.
  2. On the shelf above, the **"HONEY"** jar has tipped over on its side.
  3. Sticky honey is dripping out of the jar, running down the edge of the shelf, down the back of the books, and dripping directly into a full laundry basket of clothes on the floor.
* **Inference:** The subject should connect the boy's climbing attempt, the tipping jar, and the honey dripping onto the clean laundry.
